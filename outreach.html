ABOUT

The weSTEM Conference for High School Scientists is a collaborative, interdisciplinary, and locally engaged initiative, designed to promote diversity and female presence in the STEM fields in and beyond the United Arab Emirates by helping high school girls to prepare for successful college studies and a career in STEM.

The movement to increase female presence in the STEM fields revolves around two main areas of interest: first, ensuring a pipeline of women for technical fields by fostering STEM enthusiasm and skills in girls at an early age; and second, fixing the current “leaky pipeline” by supporting young women in their later STEM studies. It is essential not only that girls develop the necessary passion at an early age, but also that they are able to follow that passion and gain the adequate knowledge and competencies to be successful in their chosen field.

The 2016 weSTEM Conference for High School Scientists is focusing focus on developing basic skill sets for girls who aspire to work in STEM. The conference will consist of a series of sessions focusing on career development within the STEM field as well as workshops that teach basic coding and lab­work, and a competition meant to encourage teamwork and collaboration. This year’s conference will serve as a pilot project for an annual conference that aims to advance STEM exposure for high school students.
